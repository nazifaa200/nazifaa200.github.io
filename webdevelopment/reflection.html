Reflection

My undergraduate experience is what has brought me to this point today. For undergrad I chose to major in Childhood Education because I enjoyed working with children. I wanted to help them learn and grow as people who go on to be successful in their futures. I wanted to play a part in making a difference for them in school so they can make positive changes when they leave school. I really enjoyed the teaching part of this field and especially the moment where their face would light up when they understood a topic. The reason I switched from Education to Instructional Design in graduate school is because I wanted to move away from the classroom setting while I still create and teach content.


I have been in the Learning Technologies graduate program for three semesters now and I have learned so much. I was nervous starting off the first semester as I am new to all of the content and to this field. Every course has taught me new skills to carry with me. I am enjoying getting a chance to practice the skills with the assignments in the courses. I really liked this program for the portfolio requirement to graduate. It is a great way for us to build our portfolio during school so we can use it for job opportunities.


With this program I hope to go into UX and UI Design. Out of all of the paths that this degree gives me a chance to explore, I liked this one the most. I had fun with UX and UI Design in the courses here. For my career I am going to aim to start with UX design and see how that goes. After trying that out I might explore more instructional work with training or educational content.


My background in Education has made me view instructional design with the audience as the primary focus. In Education we have to focus on the students, what they need, and what will help them succeed. Every student is different and we had to approach each of them differently. Depending on the student’s background and knowledge we would approach their problems with solutions. No one solution fit every student because they all had different needs and learning styles. The same thing can be applied to the users in instructional design. Every user is different, we cannot apply the same solution to multiple types of users. I will go into instructional design by keeping the users’ needs at the top. The most important thing is utilizing design to come up with a solution to their problem. The users’ needs have to be top priority in a project and I can best do that by viewing everything from their perspective. We need to see from a user’s point of view to successfully carry out a project.




Nazifa Ahmed
